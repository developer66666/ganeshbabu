 When we need Data from Multiple Table we perform Join Operation

Inner Join

Equi Join 	- 	Matched Records
Outer Join  - 	Matched Records + Unmatched Record.
	Right Outer Join  - Macthed Record + Unmatched Record From Right Side Table.
	Left Outer Join	  - Matched Record + Unmatched Record From Left Side Table.
	Full Outer Join   - Matched Record + Unmatched Record From Both Table.

Equi JOINS

SELECT s.s_name , s.c_id , c.c_name FROM table1 s, table2 c WHERE s.c_id = c.c_id;

Outer JOINS

ROJ - Right Outer Join

SELECT s.s_name , s.c_id , c.c_name FROM table1 s RIGHT OUTER JOIN table2 c ON s.c_id = c.c_id; SELECT s.s_name , s.c_id , c.c_name FROM table1 s, table2 c WHERE s.c_id(+) = c.c_id;

LOJ - Left Outer Join

SELECT s.s_name , s.c_id , c.c_name FROM table1 s, table2 c WHERE s.c_id = c.c_id(+); SELECT s.s_name , s.c_id , c.c_name FROM table1 s LEFT OUTER JOIN table2 c ON s.c_id = c.c_id(+);

FOJ - Full Outer Join

SELECT s.s_name , s.c_id , c.c_name FROM table1 s, table2 c WHERE s.c_id = c.c_id(+) UNION SELECT s.s_name , s.c_id , c.c_name FROM table1 s, table2 c WHERE s.c_id(+) = c.c_id; SELECT s.s_name , s.c_id , c.c_name FROM table1 s FULL OUTER JOIN table2 c ON s.c_id = c.c_id;

Equi Join (Matched Records from Both Table)

SELECT employees.employee_id , employees.first_name , employees.hire_date , employees.salary , employees.department_id , departments.department_name FROM employees, departments WHERE employees.department_id = departments.department_id; SELECT e.employee_id , e.first_name , e.hire_date , e.salary , d.department_name FROM employees e, departments d WHERE e.department_id = d.department_id;

Outer Join - Matched + Unmatched Records


ROJ		-	Matched Record + Unmatched Record from Right side Table
LOJ		-	Matched Record + Unmacthed Record from Left side Table
FOJ 	-	Matched Record + Unmatched Record from Both Table.

ROJ - Matched Record + Unmatched Record from Right side Table.

SELECT e.employee_id , e.first_name , e.department_id , e.salary , d.department_name , d.manager_id FROM employees e, departments d WHERE e.department_id (+) = d.department_id; SELECT e.employee_id , e.first_name , e.department_id , e.salary , d.department_name FROM employees e RIGHT OUTER JOIN departments d ON e.department_id = d.department_id;

LOJ - Matched Record + Unmatched Record from Left side Table

SELECT e.employee_id , e.first_name , e.department_id , e.salary , d.department_name , d.manager_id FROM employees e, departments d WHERE e.department_id = d.department_id (+); SELECT e.employee_id , e.first_name , e.department_id , e.salary , d.department_name , d.manager_id FROM employees e LEFT OUTER JOIN departments d ON e.department_id = d.department_id;

FOJ - Macthed + Unmatched Record from Both Tables.

SELECT e.employee_id , e.first_name , e.department_id , e.salary , d.department_name , d.manager_id FROM employees e FULL OUTER JOIN departments d ON e.department_id = d.department_id;

No of records Returned By Join Statement.

SELECT COUNT(*) FROM employees e, departments d WHERE e.department_id = d.department_id;

Self Join (Selected Data within a Table)

Display the employees with manager name, hire_date and manager salary.

SELECT e.employee_id , e.first_name , e.hire_date , e.salary , e.manager_id , m.first_name manager_name , m.hire_date manager_hdt , m.salary manager_salary FROM employees e, employees m WHERE e.manager_id = m.employee_id;

Who are getting Salary more than their Manager Salary?

SELECT e.employee_id , e.first_name , e.hire_date , e.salary , e.manager_id , m.first_name manager_name , m.hire_date manager_hdt , m.salary manager_salary FROM employees e, employees m WHERE e.manager_id = m.employee_id AND e.salary > m.salary;

Who are all hired before Manager?

SELECT e.employee_id , e.first_name , e.hire_date , e.salary , e.manager_id , m.first_name manager_name , m.hire_date manager_hdt , m.salary manager_salary FROM employees e, employees m WHERE e.manager_id = m.employee_id AND e.hire_date < m.hire_date;

FULL Outer JOIN

-- ANSI Standard SELECT e.employee_id , e.first_name , e.hire_date , e.salary , e.department_id , d.department_name FROM employees e FULL OUTER JOIN departments d ON e.department_id = d.department_id;

SELECT employee_id , first_name , hire_date , salary , department_name FROM employees e, departments d WHERE e.department_id = d.department_id(+) AND e.department_id IS NULL; SELECT employee_id , first_name , hire_date , salary , department_name FROM employees e LEFT OUTER JOIN departments d ON e.department_id = d.department_id WHERE d.department_id IS NULL;

Write a query to get unmatched record from right side table.

SELECT employee_id , first_name , hire_date , salary , department_name FROM employees e, departments d WHERE e.department_id(+) = d.department_id AND e.department_id IS NULL; SELECT employee_id , first_name , hire_date , salary , department_name FROM employees e RIGHT OUTER JOIN departments d ON e.department_id = d.department_id AND e.department_id IS NULL;

Write a query to get unmatched record from both table.

SELECT employee_id , first_name , hire_date , salary , department_name FROM employees e FULL OUTER JOIN departments d ON e.department_id = d.department_id MINUS SELECT employee_id , first_name , hire_date , salary , department_name FROM employees e, departments d WHERE e.department_id = d.department_id; SELECT employee_id , first_name , hire_date , salary , department_name FROM employees e FULL OUTER JOIN departments d ON e.department_id = d.department_id WHERE e.department_id IS NULL OR d.department_id IS NULL;

Natural JOIN

No need to use common condition SELECT e.employee_id , e.first_name , e.hire_date , department_id , d.department_name FROM employees e NATURAL JOIN departments d; -- only 32 records will fetch bcs there are 2 column column SELECT department_id , l.street_address FROM departments d NATURAL JOIN locations l; -- only 1 common column fetch all records

Natual Join with Using Clause

SELECT d.department_id , d.department_name , location_id , l.street_address FROM departments d JOIN locations l USING(location_id);
